Chip Shortage

Nvidia's CFO predicts that chip demand will outstrip supplies for much of 2022

The high demand and low production

The biggest reason for the lack of supply is an ongoing global shortage of silicon chips. Computer chips, also called semiconductors, are mostly made from an element called silicon. Because of the pandemic, the world’s largest semiconductor manufacturers, such as Samsung, were forced to slow or halt production entirely. This stoppage in production, coupled with the rising demand for consumer electronics like computers and gaming devices also driven by the pandemic, has made it difficult for manufacturers to meet that demand.

Crypto Mining

The demand for GPUs skyrocketed when cryptocurrency mining became popular among miners because they are excellent at solving complex mathematical problems necessary to mine new coins on these blockchain networks. Mining requires an incredible amount of computing power which can be provided by powerful computers or even less expensive options that use Graphics Processing Units (GPU) instead of CPUs.

The demand has been the highest it’s been in over a year and that has led to a shortage of GPUs worldwide.

Nvidia

As initially reported by PCMag, Nvidia believes it will counteract the GPU shortage to a large degree by mid-2022. Nvidia's Chief Financial Officer Colette Kress commented at the UBS Global MTM conference yesterday that supply will begin to improve in the second half of 2022.

"The company as a whole will take the appropriate work to continue to procure more supply," Kress said. She also said that Nvidia continues to increase supply during the shortage, "We've been able to grow quite well during this year, each quarter, sequentially growing. And we do continue to plan to do that for Q4."
